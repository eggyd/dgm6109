Music Listening Hours & Happiness Across Personality Types

Hypothesis：

Different personality types may influence both how long people listen to music and how they feel while listening.

In addition to personality type and listening duration, I also included mood as a third data point. I chose to record mood because I believe the act of “choosing” music is influenced by how people feel, and people's emotions are often tied to deeper personality traits. I wanted to see whether introverted and extroverted personalities would show noticeable differences in mood after listening to music.

From the visualization results, my data supports my hypothesis about listening duration: introverted types generally listen to music for longer, while extroverted types listen for shorter periods. But the mood results didn’t match my expectations. The mood values across different personality types were actually very similar.

For the design, I chose a heart shape because music feels like an internal, emotional experience to me. It connects to both mood and personality, and a heart is the most direct symbol I could think of to represent that. The grid structure inside the heart also works well for showing units of time, making it easy to compare listening duration across personality types.

Right now, my project is still missing a feature: a hover interaction that shows the music genres the people was recorded listened to each day. I’m exploring how to add this without making the visualization feel cluttered, and I want the interaction to feel more fun and meaningful. This is the next part I plan to improve.
