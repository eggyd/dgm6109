Does Longer Music Listening Reduce Stress (I vs E)?

As listening duration increases, perceived stress tends to decrease.
Introverted listeners (blue) show a stronger stress-reduction effect compared to extroverted ones (orange).

This visualization extends the analysis by introducing a third dimension: bubble size represents stress level, while color distinguishes personality types (I vs E).
By combining these variables, the chart explores how personality traits influence emotional responses to longer music exposure.

This visualization represents one of my best works because it demonstrates a significant improvement in both my skill and visual effect. It is the first time I applied the four margin variables in D3.js to control spacing and alignment, which made the entire layout cleaner, more balanced, and easier to read. And I made a soft background rectangle to frame the main chart area, helping to visually separate the data region from the surrounding space and highlight the plotted points.
